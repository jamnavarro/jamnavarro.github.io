work playground about

Capturing unique student and alumni experiences for prospective college students

Role

Solo UI/UX Designer

Timeline

6 months

Tools

"How might we showcase unique university opportunities and programs for students to garner interest in attending Missouri University of Science & Technology?"

Background

Building the interior of the Missouri S&T Welcome Center.

Missouri S&T is a university in Rolla, MO, with degree programs across engineering, sciences, humanities, and arts fields. My team worked with the university to incorporate unique ways to engage prospective students and their families during tours and visits, and create an enjoyable experience in the lobby of their welcome center.

My role

I lead the simultaneous digital design and developer collaboration of three different interactive displays in the Welcome Center.

I was the solo UX and UI designer for the client, outlining the information architecture and working directly with the client to deliver the content design with the university's branding and digital style in mind. I prepared developer-ready design files to share with three different developers for each application and a senior designer responsible for the fabricated build pieces in the space.

aod_tablet

Nine touchscreen tablets

for users to learn about suspended pillar artifacts that represent different aspects of the Missouri S&T community

desktop_portrait

Three touchscreen vertical displays

for large-scale interactive opportunities to learn about the Missouri S&T campus and alumni map.

One large auditorium display

for automatically displaying upcoming events in the Welcome Center auditorium

Sitemaps and storytelling

I evolved sitemaps to outline the expected user journeys in the space.

To work alongside the rapid prototyping of the physical space, I outlined sitemaps for each version to more easily communicate with client and walk them through how the Welcome Center could function with digital and physical activations.

Target audience

The team pivoted quickly on early designs to best accommodate the Welcome Center's target audience.

Over the first two months of biweekly meetings, the client including a current student in participatory design sessions, and our design team focused efforts on incorporating feedback on what prospective and early college students would be most engaged with.

External user testing

For the touchscreen tablets, I built an unmoderated mid-fidelity prototype test for six participants.

The client approached me with concerns about the tablet navigation, so I conducted a test to help influence how the touchscreen tablet activation could be reworked.

Using Maze and contacting throughout my company to reach six individuals who matched the primary demographic of prospective college students and were unfamiliar with the project, I released a test that allowed free exploration, three tasks for completion, and system usability scale questions with optional open response feedback.

Simultaneous vertical and horizontal navigation was intuitive.

Users expressed feedback that they "really enjoyed the layout" and that the home screen "was pretty easy to navigate".

Layout inconsistency between some story types

Two users found the video story type to be an inconsistent layout with other story types.


Returning with three action items to minimize user confusion.

While the unmoderated testing showed promising results with the navigation, I made sure to incorporate the following to continue to avoid any potential confusion.

Include a larger touch area and allow tapping of the full card to access a story.

Incorporate a visual, animated instruction of how to navigate.

Update layouts for consistency.

Internal user testing

I personally tested active builds on test touchscreen displays before installation.
